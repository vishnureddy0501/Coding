🎯 **Core Problem**

When using **offset-based pagination** (`skip + limit`), adding/deleting records 
shifts all subsequent records, which breaks paging:

- Page 11 will either **miss one record** or **repeat one record** because the dataset changed 
  after you fetched page 10.

Even with **cursor-based pagination**, the new record will not show up in already 
loaded pages — you must **refetch page 1** or **inject it manually** to see it in 
the current session.


✅ **Approaches (Solution Names)**

1. **Offset-based Pagination (`skip/limit`)**
   - Simple, but suffers from shifting issues when data changes.
   - ❌ Not recommended for infinite scroll on large datasets.

2. **Cursor-based Pagination (✅ Recommended)**
   - Uses `cursor` or `search_after` (Mongo `_id`, `createdAt`, etc.).
   - Stable even when data changes, avoids record duplication or skipping.
   - ⚠️ Still won’t show new records unless page 1 is reloaded.

3. **Manual UI Injection + Background Refresh**
   - When you add a record, inject it into the UI immediately.
   - Optionally trigger a silent refetch of the first page to stay in sync.
   - Prevents scroll reset and gives instant feedback to the user.
